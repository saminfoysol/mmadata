Which martial art is really most effective ?

an analysis of trends in the background of top mixed martial arts contenders

about

If you've ever wanted to try a martial art but was never sure which would actually be most effective perhaps the best person to ask would be a professional fighter. To determine which disciplines are most effective, we observed the backgrounds of current UFC champions and top 5 ranked fighters from every weight division. We only counted backgrounds where the fighter has high level experience. For example, for Jiu Jitsu this means a brown belt or higher, for wrestling, at least a top ranked state competitor in high school etc.

The 2 most frequent disciplines under the backgrounds of top fighters, Jiu Jitsu and wrestling, focus on taking the fight to the ground to control the fight rather than striking on the feet.

In the chart above Muay Thai and Kickboxing are generalized under the same category. This is the most frequent discipline that focuses on striking, most likely due to its inclusion of multiple aspects of stirking such as kicking, punching, elbows, knees, and clinch fighting.

More traditional martial arts such as Tae Kwon Do and Karate have lower frequency and interestingly, 7 fighters had no significant background in any discipline, perhaps indiciating athleticism as key in fighting or indicating mixed martial arts as a sport all on its own, with strong backgrounds in other disciplines helpful but not necessary.

Breakdown of Fight Stoppages in the UFC

Although from the bar graph above one might conclude jiu jitsu must be the ultimate martial art to go with above all else, most finishes in a UFC fight are actually by knockout or technical knockout by strikes, which makes sense since all fights start on the fight and therefore often with striking. The optimal martial art to learn for self defense may even vary by body type. An analysis of finishes in the UFC reveals the higher the weight class, the higher the chance of a knockout and the lower the chance of a submission victory.
